2023/2024 Season

Season Summary

Formations

In this season I lined up with a 4-2-3-1 (2DM) formation, with a tactic inspired by Liverpool FC's title run back in 08/09. There were some minor changes in the roles that were used but the core concepts stayed the same.

As you can see in the image to the right. I have changed the deep-lying playmaker role to be more defensive, this was solely done to allow Trent Alexander-Arnold be more creative and get further up the pitch.

Competitions

At the end of the season we won every trophy available to win. Finishing the Premier League with 90 points (28W, 6D, 4L), and winning the League Cup, FA Cup and Europa League comfortably.

Transfers

Winter Window

At the start of the January transfer window after pulling a 7 point gap at the top of the Premier League, the key objective of this window was to improve the squad depth within the midfield. A injury ravaged first 6 months where at one point there were 8 first team players unavailable, reinforcements were desperately needed.

Paulo Dybala

After scouting Europe Paulo Dybala popped up on the radar, the 30 Year old had a release clause of £10.5m a bargin price for todays market. This signing had very little analysis behind and was purely decided on by the price tag of the player. In terms of how he fits into the squad he would be utilised as a squad player who could slit into the number 10 position (AMC) or as a deputy striker. A good option to have as the first 6 months of this save has been ravaged by injuries across the squad.

Xaver Schlager

A 26 Year-old Defensive Midfielder, similar to Dybala, Schlager had little analysis into the decision to bring him to the club other than he also had a relatively low release clause. For Schlager his release clause was £30m, which again is a bargin for the age and the ability of the player. In terms of how he fits into the system, he would likely slot into the left of the two defensive midfielders alongside Mac Allister, meaning that unfortunately Endo will have to take up a squad role going forward.

Analysis

Raw Data

Goalkeepers

No suprises here but Alisson was our first choice keeper this season, making 44 appearances in all compertitions, keeping 20 clean sheets along the way. Caoimhin Kelleher was our backup and cup competition goalkeeper making 18 appearances and keeping 8 clean sheets. Adrián was the emergency backup, conceeding zero goals in his two games when called upon.

All the goalkeepers had good seasons this year with all of them maintaining 7.00 Average match rating. In possession the Alisson made an completed an average of 24 passes per game, with Kelleher and Adrián completing 23 passes respectfully. Kelleher had the highest pass completion rate of 87% closely followed by Alisson with 86%, Adrián fell well below the other two keepers with a pass completion rate of 80%.

Whilst Kelleher over performed in terms of saves made this season (Save rate of 85% compared to his expected save rate of 83%) and Alisson under performed (83% Save rate, "86% expected save rate). However, Alisson maintained a lower goals conceeded per 90 minutes Vs Kelleher (Alisson: 0.66 Vs Kelleher: 0.86).

Defenders

Andrew Robertson had the highest rating this season averaging 7.17 each match, while Conor Bradley had the lowest rating of 6.83. Konaté was the most prominant defender this season making 56 appearances in all competitions followed by Trent Alexander-Arnold (51). Whilst Joe Gomez made more appearances than Andrew Robertson, Robertson completed more minutes (3279 vs 2386), showing this season Gomez was utilised more as a squad player and impact substitution.
Going forward the defenders as a collective contributed 10 goals this season. The Full backs also created 29 goals from the wider areas with both Trent and Robertson creating 10 goals each.
Defensively suprisingly the full backs more tackles than the center backs, with Robertson and Tsimikas making on average 3 tackles per match on the left hand side of defence and Trent and Gomez making 2.49 and 1.36 respectively. The full backs did attempt more tackles than the center half, likely due to the tactics selected asking the team to press higher up the pitch. As expected the center backs were the arially superior with van Dijk and Konaté making 8 arial challenges per 90 minutes. This center back pairing also made 90 key headers combined across the whole season, showing how crucial they were to the quadruple season. In the air Konaté was more dominant than van Dijk this season, completing 84% of his headers compared to van Dijks 76%.
With the high pressing style of play, the fullbacks made more interceptions in compared to the center backs, on average completing over 3 interceptions per match. However, Trent Alexander-Arnold fell behind in this area only making on average 2.67 placing him 6th when ranked among other defenders. The fullbacks also made the most fouls with Robertson and Alexander-Arnold both making over 50 fouls over the season, resulting in both players collecting 21 yellow cards between them (Trent 13, Robertson 8).

Midfielders

In the midfield despite Xaver Schlager joining in the January transfer window, Endo still maintiained the highest appearances this season with 56 in all competitions, with Alexis Mac Allister making one appearance less.
There was alot of rotation in the midfield in this season with 7 of the 10 midfielders making over 20 appearances in this season. Thiago, Harvey Elliott and Stefan Bajčetić all had injury ravaged season. Harvey Elliott in particular, had a knee ligament injury early on in the season resulting in him missing most of the season and only featuring in 10 matches this season in all competitions.
Of the midfielders who made over 20 appearances, Szoboszlai had the highest average match rating (7.14) where he contributed 13 goals and 10 assists from the midfield. Mac Allister was a close second maintaining an average rating of 7.06, he also contributed 13 goals from deeper in midfield as well as 4 assists.
Suprisingly Endo playing in the ball winning midfielder role assisted 8 goals across the season, he was also the most tidy in possession completing 88% of his passes with only Curtis Jones having a higher pass completion rate (89%). January signing Paulo Dybala also made his presence in the squad contributing 6 goals and 3 assists in 23 games. Whist he had a low pass completion rate (83%) relative to the rest of the squad, he had the highest chances created per 90 minutes of 0.72. He also had the most shots on target per 90 minutes of the squad with 1.23. Being more attacking minded than Szoboszlai, Dybala also had the highest Key passes per 90 minutes averaging 2.67, with Szoboszlai only making 1.73 key passes per 90.

Forwards

101 goals scored across all of the first team forwards this season, with an season xG of 79.77. The forwards were ruthless in front on goal, scoring 21 goals more than expected this season. The majority of the goals came from Diogo Jota who scored 34 goals across 56 matches in all competitions. Darwin Nunez was runner up with 23 goals in 55 appearances.
Mo Salah had a quiet season with the African Cup of Nations and some injuries he made 28 appearances, scoring 10 goals and assisting 6.
In the creative department, Cody Gakpo lead the key passes with 58 across the season, with Luis Díaz coming second with 46 key passes.
Ben Doak made a suprising stake for a more playing time next season, making 18 appearances this season scoring 3 goals but impressively notching 8 assists, he lead the charts for chances created per 90 minutes with 1.48, the closest player to him was Cody Gakpo with 0.80 chances created per 90. He had the lowest shots per 90 minutes showing that going forward he prefers to pass to teammates rather than take shots himself. The right winger also ranked second in average distance ran per 90 minutes, running 14.1km with only Mo Salah (14.4km) ahead of him.
